## **“Avamaanam (అవమానం)” meaning in English**

**Avamaanam means insult, humiliation, or loss of respect.**

It refers to a situation where someone speaks or behaves in a way that lowers our dignity or makes us feel disrespected.

---
## Simple meaning

* Loss of respect
* Being looked down upon by others
* Hurt to self-respect
* Feeling insulted or embarrassed

---

##  Examples

### 1) Insult through words

* Someone says: “You can’t do anything.”
   This is avamaanam (an insult)

---

### 2) Public humiliation

* A teacher says in front of the class:
  “You always fail.”
   This is humiliation

---

### 3) Ignoring someone

* Someone intentionally ignores you while you are speaking
   This also feels like avamaanam (disrespect)

---

### 4) Undermining someone’s ability

* “This task is not for your level, you can’t do it.”
   This is also avamaanam (belittling someone)

---

##  Simple idea

**Avamaanam means: when someone damages your dignity or respect.**

## 1) “Himsiste” (హింసిస్తే)

### Meaning in English:

 **“If someone tortures / hurts / abuses / causes violence.”**

### Explanation:

“Himsiste” comes from the idea of **violence or harm**. It refers to a situation where someone intentionally causes physical or mental suffering to another person.

---

### Examples:

* Physically hitting someone
  → This is “himsiste” (physical violence)

* Threatening or scaring someone repeatedly
  → This is also “himsiste” (mental abuse)

* Forcing someone into pain or suffering
  → This is also included

---

### Simple idea:

 “Himsiste” means **causing harm, violence, or suffering to someone deliberately.**

---

## 2) “Anyayamga Chedu maatalu palikithe” (అన్యాయంగా చెడు మాటలు పలికితే)

### Meaning in English:

 **“If someone speaks bad or unfair words unjustly.”**

---

### Explanation:

This refers to when a person **uses wrong, harmful, or unfair speech against someone else**, especially without reason or justice.

It includes:

* False accusations
* Harsh insults
* Unfair criticism
* Words that damage someone’s dignity or reputation

---

### Examples:

* Saying something false like: “He is a thief” without proof
  → This is unjust harmful speech

* Insulting someone without reason
  → This is unfair bad speech

* Spreading lies about someone
  → This is also included

---

### Simple idea:

“Anyayamga chedu maatalu palikithe” means **using unjust or harmful words that damage another person’s respect or truth.**

---

## Final simple summary:

* **Avamaanam** → insult / humiliation (loss of respect)
* **Himsiste** → causing harm, violence, or suffering
* **Anyayamga chedu maatalu palikithe** → speaking unfair or harmful words against someone

## “Adhyatmikamga deenuluga unna vallade Devuni rajyam” meaning

**English meaning:**

**“The Kingdom of God belongs to those who are spiritually humble.”**

---

##  Clear explanation

This sentence means:

God’s kingdom (or divine truth/reality) is not for proud or ego-filled people,
but for those who are **humble in spirit, simple in heart, and not full of ego**.

---

##  Word breakdown

* **Adhyatmikamga** → spiritually / in a spiritual sense
* **Deenuluga unna** → being humble / meek / lowly in heart
* **Vallade** → those people only / belongs to those people
* **Devuni rajyam** → Kingdom of God

---

##  Simple idea

**God’s kingdom belongs to people who are spiritually humble, not arrogant or self-centered.**

---

##  Deeper meaning (easy understanding)

It is not talking mainly about money or status.

It is talking about inner qualities like:

* humility
* simplicity
* openness to truth
* lack of pride

---

## One-line summary

 **The Kingdom of God is for those who are humble in spirit.**
